# PC Assembly Study Notes

## 1. Power Supply Unit (PSU)

### Selection Criteria
- **Wattage**: Calculate the total power consumption of all components, then choose a PSU with **at least 25% more** capacity than that total.
  - Ensures stability under load and leaves headroom for future upgrades.
- **Compatibility**:
  - Confirm the PSU form factor fits your **case** and matches your **motherboard** standard.
  - Verify the PSU provides all necessary **connectors** for your components (motherboard 24-pin, CPU 8-pin, GPU power, SATA, etc.).

### Installation Steps
1. Open the side panel of the case by removing the screws.
2. Slide the PSU into its designated bay.
3. Align the screw holes on the PSU with those on the case and secure with screws.
4. **Check the voltage switch** (if present) — set it to the correct regional voltage (e.g., 220V in South Korea).

### Key Rules
- **Never force a connector.** All connectors are keyed to fit only one way. If it resists, check the orientation and inspect for bent pins.
- Tighten screws gradually in a cross pattern for even seating.

## 2. CPU (Central Processing Unit)

### Installation Steps
1. **Orient the CPU**: Find the small **triangle marking** on one corner of the CPU. Match it to the identical triangle on the motherboard socket.
2. **Place the CPU**: Gently lower the CPU straight down into the socket — do **not** press or force it.
3. **Lock it in**: Close the socket cover and press down the retention lever to lock the CPU firmly in place.
4. **Apply thermal paste**: Place a **pea-sized drop** of thermal paste at the center of the CPU surface. This ensures efficient heat transfer to the heat sink.
5. **Install the heat sink**: Position the heat sink (cooler) over the CPU, aligning it with the mounting holes around the socket.
6. **Secure the heat sink**: Tighten the four screws in a **diagonal (cross) pattern** — tighten one corner, then the opposite corner, then the remaining two. This ensures a flush, even contact between the CPU and heat sink.

### Key Rules
- The triangle orientation is critical — a misaligned CPU can bend pins and cause permanent damage.
- Diagonal tightening spreads thermal paste evenly and prevents uneven pressure.
- **Never force the CPU into the socket.**

## 3. RAM (Memory Module)

### Installation Steps
1. **Open the levers**: Push the retention clips on both sides of the RAM slot outward until they click open.
2. **Align the notch**: Match the small notch (gap) on the RAM module's connector edge with the raised key inside the slot. This prevents incorrect insertion.
3. **Press down vertically**: Using both thumbs, press the RAM straight down into the slot with firm, even pressure.
4. **Confirm the click**: The retention clips will snap back into place automatically, locking the module in. A click sound confirms correct installation.

### Key Rules
- Install RAM **before** mounting the motherboard into the case — it is much harder to do afterward due to limited space.
- If the module does not go in, **check the notch alignment** — do not force it.
- For dual-channel performance with two sticks, check the motherboard manual for the correct slot pairing (usually slots 2 and 4, or A2/B2).

## Summary Table

| Component | Critical Step | Common Mistake |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **PSU** | Match wattage (×1.25), check connectors | Forcing connectors, wrong voltage |
| **CPU** | Align triangle, diagonal screw tightening | Wrong orientation, forcing into socket |
| **RAM** | Align notch, press until click | Not pressing firmly enough, wrong slot |

---

> **Golden Rule for all assembly**: If something requires excessive force, **stop and check**. All components are designed to fit naturally when properly aligned.
