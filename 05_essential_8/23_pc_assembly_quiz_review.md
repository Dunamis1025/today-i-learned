# Chapter 2: PC Assembly — Study Notes

## 1. Power Supply Unit (PSU)
- Converts **AC** (wall outlet) → **DC** (computer components)
- Outputs: **3.3V / 5V / 12V**
- Before buying, check:
  - **Case form factor** (must physically fit)
  - **Total wattage** of all components (PSU capacity must exceed total draw)
- The wattage rating (e.g. "550W") = **maximum output power**

---

## 2. Motherboard & CPU
- Motherboard **form factor** must match the case
- CPU socket types:
  - **Intel** → LGA socket
  - **AMD** → PGA socket
- Socket on CPU and motherboard **must match**

---

## 3. RAM
- Must be compatible with the motherboard (check manual or manufacturer website **before purchasing**)
- RAM speed must be supported by the chipset
- Hand-tighten screws before using a screwdriver (applies to all components)

---

## 4. Storage Drives
| Type | Use Case | Size |
|------|----------|------|
| HDD | Desktop (high capacity) | **3.5 inch** |
| SSD/HDD | Laptop | **2.5 inch** |
| Optical Drive | CD/DVD | 5.25 inch |

- SATA cable connects to **SATA ports** on the motherboard (Section D)

---

## 5. Adapter / Expansion Cards
| Card | Purpose | Key Consideration |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| **Graphics Card** | Display output | PCIe slot |
| **Sound Card** | Audio processing | **DSP** (Digital Signal Processor) quality |
| **RAID Card** | Data fault tolerance | Protects data if a drive fails |
| **NIC** | Network connection | Upgrade for more bandwidth or Wi-Fi |
| **Capture Card** | Record video input | — |

- Inserted into **PCI** or **PCIe** slots on the motherboard

---

## 6. NIC (Network Interface Card)
Reasons to upgrade:
- **Increase bandwidth** (e.g. 100Mbps → 1Gbps)
- **Add wireless (Wi-Fi) connectivity**

---

## 7. Media / Storage Cards
| Card | Status | Common Use |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **microSD** | Current standard | Smartphones, small devices |
| **CompactFlash** | Older but still used | Professional video cameras |
| **miniSD** | Obsolete | — |
| **xD** | Discontinued | Was Olympus-exclusive |

---

## 8. Front Panel Connectors
| Connector | Pin Count |
|-----------|-----------|
| Power Button / LED | 1–2 pins (individual) |
| Drive Activity LED | 1–2 pins |
| **USB** | **9–10 pins, 2 rows** |

---

## 9. Safety Rules
- **Remove watches and jewelry** before working inside a PC (metal causes short circuits)
- **Bend at the knees** when lifting heavy equipment (not your back)
- **Hand-tighten screws first**, then finish with a screwdriver

---
