# [Networking Fundamentals] Physical Layer & Cabling - Practice Questions

## Question 09: Frame Encoding
- **Question:** Which statement correctly describes frame encoding?
- **Answer:** **It converts bits into a predefined code in order to provide a predictable pattern to help distinguish data bits from control bits.**
- **Key Concept:** Encoding is the process of converting data into a pattern (code) that the receiver can recognize. This pattern allows the system to differentiate between the actual payload (data bits) and control signals (like start/end delimiters).

## Question 10: UTP Cabling Characteristics
- **Question:** What is a characteristic of UTP cabling?
- **Answer:** **Cancellation**
- **Key Concept:** UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair) uses pairs of wires twisted together. This design creates opposing magnetic fields that **cancel** out noise, electromagnetic interference (EMI), and crosstalk from adjacent wires.

## Question 11: Wireless Interference
- **Question:** A wireless LAN signal is occasionally affected by some type of interference. What is a possible cause of the signal distortion?
- **Answer:** **The microwave oven**
- **Key Concept:** Most Wi-Fi networks and **microwave ovens** both operate on the **2.4 GHz** frequency band. When a microwave is running, it can leak radiation that distorts or jams wireless signals nearby.

## Question 12: OSI Physical Layer Purpose
- **Question:** What is the purpose of the OSI physical layer?
- **Answer:** **Transmitting bits across the local media**
- **Key Concept:** The Physical Layer (Layer 1) focuses on hardware and signal transmission. It accepts a stream of **bits** (0s and 1s) from the Data Link Layer and converts them into electrical, optical, or radio signals for transmission.

## Question 13: Understanding Crosstalk
- **Question:** Which characteristic describes crosstalk?
- **Answer:** **The distortion of the transmitted messages from signals carried in adjacent wires**
- **Key Concept:** Crosstalk is a specific type of interference where the signal from one wire "bleeds" into an **adjacent** wire due to electromagnetic coupling. This leads to signal distortion and data errors.

## Question 14: Network Performance - Throughput
- **Question:** What is indicated by the term throughput?
- **Answer:** **The measure of the bits transferred across the media over a given period of time**
- **Key Concept:** - **Bandwidth:** The maximum theoretical capacity.
  - **Throughput:** The **actual** amount of data transferred in a specific timeframe.
  - **Goodput:** The measure of usable, successful data (minus overhead).

## Question 15: Wireless Standards Organizations
- **Question:** Which standards organization oversees development of wireless LAN standards?
- **Answer:** **IEEE**
- **Key Concept:** The **IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers)** maintains the **802.11** family of standards, which are the technical specifications for Wi-Fi.
